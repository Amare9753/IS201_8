Consistency

Stick to a consistent sleep schedule: Try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. This helps regulate your body's internal clock and can make it easier to fall asleep and wake up feeling rested.

Environment

Create a sleep-conducive environment: Make sure your sleep environment is cool, dark, and quiet. Use blackout curtains or an eye mask to block out light, earplugs or a white noise machine to block out noise, and set the temperature to a comfortable level.

Sleep Prep

Avoid stimulating activities before bed: Avoid doing stimulating activities like working on your computer, watching TV, or playing video games before bed. These activities can make it harder to fall asleep by keeping your brain active and alert. Instead, try reading a book, taking a warm bath, or doing some relaxation exercises to help prepare your body and mind for sleep.
